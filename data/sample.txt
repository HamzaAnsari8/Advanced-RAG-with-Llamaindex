Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), and Deep Learning (DL) form a hierarchical, nested structure representing the evolution of computing from rule-based systems to autonomous learning. AI is the broad concept of machines simulating human intelligence, ML is a subset that enables machines to learn from data, and DL is a specialized subset of ML using multi-layered neural networks for complex pattern recognition. 



Artificial Intelligence (AI): The Umbrella Concept
Definition: The theory and development of computer systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as reasoning, decision-making, and perception.
Approach: Can be symbolic (rule-based) or connectionist (learning-based).
Goal: To create systems that can sense, reason, act, and adapt.

Machine Learning (ML): Learning from Data
Definition: A subset of AI focused on algorithms that learn patterns from data to make predictions or decisions without being explicitly programmed for every rule.
Key Techniques:
Supervised Learning: Training on labeled data.
Unsupervised Learning: Finding hidden patterns in unlabeled data.
Reinforcement Learning: Learning through trial and error to maximize rewards.
Usage: Statistical models like linear regression, decision trees, and support vector machines.

Deep Learning (DL): Neural Networks 
Definition: A specialized subset of ML, inspired by the structure of the human brain (artificial neural networks).
Methodology: Uses deep layers (multiple hidden layers) of nodes to process unstructured data (images, text, audio).
Types: Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for images, Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs/LSTMs) for sequential data, and Transformers.
Advantage: High accuracy with large datasets, but requires significant computational power (GPUs).

Key Differences and Relationship
Hierarchy: DL is a subset of ML, which is a subset of AI.
Data Requirement: DL requires vast amounts of data, while ML can work with smaller, structured datasets.
Feature Engineering: ML often requires manual feature extraction, while DL automatically discovers features.
Applications: AI (General), ML (Fraud detection, recommendations), DL (Self-driving cars, image recognition).
